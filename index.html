General Questions asked by Customers

What are bonds and debentures?

Bonds and debentures are debt securities with a fixed rate of return and fixed maturity period. Bonds are issued by governments, while debentures are issued by corporations, to secure loans from investors for business purposes. Periodic interest payments are made to bond/debenture holders and the principal amount is repaid upon maturity. They offer a reliable source of income and portfolio diversification, but risk should be evaluated before investing.

What is the process for investment through Bondspe?

Investing with Bondspe is a simple process, starting with verifying your identity through KYC (Know Your Customer). Bondspe takes care of processing all the necessary documents for you, ensuring a hassle-free investment experience. To purchase Bonds/Debentures, you'll need to transfer the investment amount to the counterparty settlement authorities (ICCL/NSCCL) Bank Account held with the Reserve Bank of India. The transfer must be made using RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement) mode. Once the transaction is complete, the units will be transferred to your Demat account.

How to find DP ID and Client ID?

Your DP ID and Client ID are important details for your Demat account. They can be easily found in the profile section of your broking account or in your E-CAS statement sent to your email. If your Demat account is with CDSL, both the DP ID and Client ID will be 8 digits each. For example, if your Demat number is 0577057744224422, then your DP ID is 05770577 and your Client ID is 44224422. If your Demat account is with NSDL, both the DP ID and Client ID will be 8 characters each, starting within followed by numbers. For example, if your Demat number is IN12345678912345, then your DP ID is IN123456 and your Client ID is 78912345.

How long will it take for the units to reflect in my account?

After you complete your payment, the units will usually be transferred to your Demat account on the same day. However, in rare cases, the units may not appear in your Demat account immediately. If this happens, you can ask your broker for a consolidated holding statement to check the status of your transfer.
